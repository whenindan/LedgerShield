# MASTER SERVICE AGREEMENT

**Agreement Number:** MSA-2024-0312  
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2024  
**Expiration Date:** December 31, 2025  

---

## PARTIES

This Master Service Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of the Effective Date above by and between:

**Acme Corp**, a Delaware corporation with its principal place of business at 4800 Technology Drive, Suite 200, Austin, TX 78701 ("Vendor");

and

**ACASO Inc.**, a Delaware corporation with its principal place of business at 101 Market Street, Suite 1400, San Francisco, CA 94105 ("Client").

Vendor and Client are each referred to herein individually as a "Party" and collectively as the "Parties."

---

## 1. SCOPE OF SERVICES

1.1 **Platform Access.** Vendor shall provide Client with access to the Acme Corp SaaS platform ("Platform"), including the Acme Data API ("API"), the web-based management console, and all associated documentation, in accordance with the terms set forth herein.

1.2 **API Usage.** Client is authorized to make API calls to Vendor's production endpoints as described in Exhibit A. API access is provisioned on a per-call metered basis. Vendor will provide monthly usage reports no later than the fifth (5th) business day following the close of each calendar month.

1.3 **Support.** Vendor shall provide standard technical support via email (support@acmecorp.com) during business hours (9:00 AM – 6:00 PM CT, Monday through Friday, excluding federal holidays). Response time SLA for Priority-1 issues is four (4) business hours.

---

## 2. PRICING AND PERMITTED BILLING CATEGORIES

2.1 **API Call Rate.** API calls shall be billed at a rate not to exceed **$0.012 per call** ("API Call Ceiling Rate"). Any rate in excess of the API Call Ceiling Rate is invalid and shall not be payable by Client without a written amendment to this Agreement executed by authorized signatories of both Parties.

2.2 **Permitted Billing Categories.** Vendor may invoice Client only for the following categories of charges:
   - (a) API calls, billed at or below the API Call Ceiling Rate per Section 2.1;
   - (b) Overage fees, subject to the conditions in Section 2.3;
   - (c) Professional services, if separately authorized in writing via a Statement of Work.

2.3 **Overage Fees.** Any fees characterized as overages, above-threshold usage surcharges, or burst pricing require Vendor to obtain **prior written approval** from Client's designated billing contact before such charges may appear on any invoice. Invoices containing unapproved overage charges shall be deemed disputed and Client shall have no obligation to pay such amounts pending resolution.

2.4 **Excluded Billing Categories.** The billing category labeled **"Platform Maintenance Fee"** is expressly **not a permitted billing category** under this Agreement. Vendor shall not invoice Client for any charge bearing this label or any substantially similar label. Client may reject, without penalty, any invoice line item purporting to bill for platform maintenance, infrastructure upkeep, hosting overhead, or any analogous category not enumerated in Section 2.2.

---

## 3. PAYMENT TERMS

3.1 **Net-30 Payment.** Client shall remit payment for all valid, undisputed invoices within **thirty (30) calendar days** of the invoice date ("Net-30"). Invoices submitted with errors, unauthorized charges, or discrepancies as defined in this Agreement are considered disputed and the Net-30 clock shall not commence until a corrected invoice is issued.

3.2 **Invoice Requirements.** All invoices must include: (a) Agreement Number MSA-2024-0312, (b) itemized line items with per-unit rates, (c) the billing period, and (d) Vendor's banking or remittance instructions.

3.3 **Late Fees.** Late fees shall not accrue on disputed amounts. On undisputed amounts paid after the Net-30 window, a late fee of 1.5% per month may apply.

---

## 4. DISPUTE RESOLUTION

4.1 **Billing Disputes.** Either Party may dispute an invoice by delivering written notice to the other Party within fifteen (15) days of receipt, citing the specific line items in dispute and referencing this Agreement (MSA-2024-0312). The Parties agree to negotiate in good faith to resolve billing disputes within thirty (30) days of the dispute notice.

4.2 **Escalation.** Disputes not resolved at the billing-contact level within thirty (30) days shall be escalated to each Party's respective Vice President of Finance. If unresolved after an additional fifteen (15) days, either Party may pursue binding arbitration under the JAMS Streamlined Arbitration Rules, with venue in San Francisco County, California.

4.3 **Governing Law.** This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the State of California, without regard to its conflict-of-law provisions.

---

## 5. TERM AND TERMINATION

5.1 This Agreement commences on the Effective Date and remains in effect through the Expiration Date unless earlier terminated. Either Party may terminate for material breach upon thirty (30) days written notice if the breach is not cured within that period.
